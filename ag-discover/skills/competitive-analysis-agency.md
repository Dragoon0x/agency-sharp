# Competitive Analysis for Clients
**Purpose:** Analyze the client's competitive landscape to inform design, positioning, and differentiation — not a generic SWOT but specific visual and experience insights that drive creative decisions.

Agency competitive analysis is different from business competitive analysis. You're not analyzing market share or business models — you're analyzing the visual language, user experience, messaging, content strategy, and digital presence of competitors to find opportunities for differentiation.

## The Analysis Framework
For each competitor (5-8): capture their homepage, key conversion pages, and mobile experience. Evaluate: visual identity (color, type, imagery style), messaging (headline structure, value proposition framing, tone), UX patterns (navigation, information architecture, key flows), content strategy (blog, resources, social presence), and technical execution (performance, accessibility, mobile optimization).

## The Insight Extraction
The goal is not a comparison table. It's strategic insight. What visual conventions does the industry follow? (These are user expectations you should respect.) Where do all competitors look the same? (This is your differentiation opportunity.) Where do competitors fail? (This is your quality advantage.) What has no competitor attempted? (This is your innovation space.)

## The Deliverable
A 15-20 page analysis with: competitive landscape overview, pattern analysis (what everyone does), gap analysis (what no one does), opportunity map (where the client can differentiate), and specific recommendations for the creative direction.

## When to use
During discovery for any brand, web, or product project. When the client is entering a competitive market. When the client's current presence is indistinguishable from competitors.
