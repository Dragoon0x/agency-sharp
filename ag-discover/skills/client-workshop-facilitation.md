# Client Workshop Facilitation
**Purpose:** Facilitate workshops that produce alignment, insights, and decisions in 2-4 hours — the concentrated working session that replaces weeks of back-and-forth.

Workshops are the agency's most powerful alignment tool. In 3 hours, a well-facilitated workshop can produce the alignment that would take 6 weeks of emails and meetings. The key is structure: every minute is planned, every exercise has a clear output, and the facilitator keeps the room moving.

## The Workshop Structure
Opening (15 min): context, objectives, ground rules. Exercise 1 (45 min): divergent thinking — generate ideas broadly (brainstorming, card sorting, competitive review). Exercise 2 (45 min): convergent thinking — narrow and prioritize (voting, clustering, forced ranking). Break (15 min). Exercise 3 (45 min): definition — define the direction (articulating principles, sketching concepts, writing statements). Closing (15 min): recap decisions, next steps, assignments.

## The Facilitation Rules
One conversation at a time. Write everything visibly (whiteboard, Miro, FigJam). Vote instead of debate for preference decisions. Silence is productive — include individual thinking time before group discussion. The facilitator does not contribute opinions — they manage the process.

## The Output
The workshop should produce a specific artifact: a brief, a positioning statement, a set of design principles, a prioritized feature list, or a user journey map. If the workshop doesn't produce a tangible output that drives the next phase, it was a discussion, not a workshop.

## When to use
At the start of every strategic engagement. When stakeholders are misaligned. When the client can't articulate what they want. When email threads have gone circular without resolution.
