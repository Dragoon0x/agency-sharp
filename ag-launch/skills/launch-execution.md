# Launch Execution
**Purpose:** Execute the launch with the coordination, timing, and communication that makes go-live smooth — because a launch is not just deploying code, it's a coordinated event involving multiple stakeholders.

A launch has three audiences: the client (who needs to feel confident), the users (who need a working product), and the team (who needs clear coordination). Managing all three simultaneously requires a launch plan that accounts for timing, communication, contingency, and the specific go-live steps.

## The Launch Plan
T-5 days: Final QA complete, staging approved by client, all content final. T-3 days: DNS changes prepared (if applicable), monitoring configured, rollback plan documented. T-1 day: Final staging review, launch checklist verified, all stakeholders confirmed. T-0: Deploy to production, verify critical paths, monitor for errors, send go-live confirmation. T+1 day: Monitor analytics, address any issues, send post-launch report to client.

## The Communication Plan
Client: daily updates in the final week. Launch confirmation within 30 minutes of go-live. Post-launch monitoring report at end of day. Team: clear task assignments for launch day. On-call schedule for the first 48 hours. Escalation path for critical issues.

## The Rollback Plan
Every launch needs a rollback plan: if something goes catastrophically wrong, how do you revert to the previous state? Document the steps, test them in staging, and know who has the authority to trigger a rollback. Having a plan you never use is better than needing a plan you don't have.

## When to use
Every launch. When establishing the agency's standard launch process. When past launches have been chaotic or error-prone.
