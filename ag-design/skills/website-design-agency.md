# Website Design for Agencies
**Purpose:** Design websites that achieve client business objectives — not portfolio pieces that win design awards but business tools that convert, communicate, and perform.

Agency website design has a dual audience: the client's business objectives and the agency's portfolio. The best work serves both — a website that achieves its business goals (conversion, communication, lead generation) while demonstrating exceptional craft. When these conflict, the client's business objectives win.

## The Agency Website Process
Content-first: the structure and content must be defined before design begins. Designing empty layouts and filling in content later produces websites where the design and content fight each other. Content drives layout. Layout does not drive content.

Mobile-first: design the mobile experience first, then expand to desktop. This forces prioritization — mobile has less space, so only the essential elements make the cut. Desktop becomes the enhanced version, not the default.

Component-based: design reusable sections (hero, features, testimonials, CTA, FAQ) that work together as a system. This approach makes the design scalable, the development more efficient, and the client able to build new pages from existing components.

## The Quality Bar
Every agency website should meet: 90+ Lighthouse performance score, WCAG AA accessibility, responsive across all common breakpoints (375px, 768px, 1024px, 1440px), and SEO fundamentals (proper heading structure, meta descriptions, OG images). These are not nice-to-haves — they're the baseline for professional agency work.

## When to use
Every website project. When establishing quality standards for web design delivery. When websites aren't performing as expected post-launch.
