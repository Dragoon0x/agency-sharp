# Job Description Agency
**Purpose:** Apply job description agency with the rigor and specificity that profitable agency delivery demands — where client expectations, fixed budgets, and quality standards leave no room for guesswork.

## Why This Matters

Agency scaling is not linear growth — it's a series of phase transitions where everything that worked at the previous stage breaks. The org structure that works at 5 breaks at 15. The sales process that works at $500K breaks at $2M. The founder's role changes at every stage. Anticipating these transitions is what separates agencies that grow from agencies that plateau.

For job description agency specifically, the gap between doing this well and doing it poorly is the gap between a profitable project and a money-losing one. Agencies that systematize job description agency deliver more consistently, scope more accurately, and retain clients longer than agencies that handle it ad-hoc.

## The Framework

The Founder Role Evolution: Maker → Manager → Leader → CEO. At each transition, the founder must let go of the previous role's activities — and most founders resist because the previous role is what they're good at. A founder who keeps designing at the Manager stage is a bottleneck, not an asset.

## How to Apply This

**Step 1: Assess the current state.** Before changing anything, document how job description agency is currently handled. What works? What breaks? Where does the team spend unnecessary time? The assessment reveals whether the problem is process, people, tools, or all three.

**Step 2: Define the standard.** What does good look like for job description agency in this agency, for this project type, at this scale? The standard should be specific enough that two team members would independently produce similar outputs. If the standard is vague, the results will be inconsistent.

**Step 3: Build the system.** Create the templates, checklists, documentation, and workflows that make the standard repeatable. The system should be lightweight enough to actually use and comprehensive enough to catch the common failure modes.

**Step 4: Measure and improve.** Track whether the system is working. The key metrics for job description agency: Revenue growth rate, profit margin by stage, employee retention rate, client retention rate, revenue per employee, utilization rate, pipeline coverage ratio. Review quarterly. Adjust based on what the data shows, not what feels right.

## Real-World Use Cases

**Early-stage agency (1-5 people):** At this scale, job description agency is often handled by the founder directly. The priority is documenting what works before the team grows. Create a simple version — a one-page checklist or template — that captures the founder's judgment so it can be delegated.

**Growth-stage agency (5-20 people):** At this scale, job description agency must be delegated and systematized. The founder can't be in every loop. Build the standard, train the team, and create quality checks that catch problems without creating bottlenecks. The goal is consistent output without the founder's involvement in every instance.

**Scaled agency (20+ people):** At this scale, job description agency needs governance — who owns the standard, how it's updated, how new team members are trained, and how exceptions are handled. The system should be self-maintaining: documented enough that a new PM or lead can manage it without institutional knowledge from the founders.

## Common Mistakes

**Not evolving the founder's role — staying in the maker role when the agency needs a manager.** This is the most common failure mode and the one that costs the most in rework, client friction, and margin erosion.

**Growing all services equally — instead of doubling down on the most profitable and differentiated service.** This usually happens when the agency is moving too fast to follow the process — which is exactly when the process matters most.

**Ignoring culture during growth — culture degrades silently until it's a retention crisis.** This compounds over time. Each instance is small. The cumulative effect is significant.

## The Agency-Specific Dimension

Agency work has constraints that product teams and freelancers don't face. Multiple clients means job description agency must work across different brands, industries, and stakeholder dynamics. Parallel projects mean the team handles job description agency for 3-5 clients simultaneously. Fixed budgets mean there's no room for the process to be inefficient. External deadlines mean the timeline is real. These constraints make systematizing job description agency not optional but essential.

When job description agency is the current bottleneck in delivery or operations. When quality in this area is inconsistent across projects or team members. When the agency is scaling and needs job description agency to work without the founder in the loop. When post-project retrospectives identify this as a recurring issue.
