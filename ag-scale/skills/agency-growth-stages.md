# Agency Growth Stages
**Purpose:** Understand the inflection points in agency growth — because the practices that work at $500K break at $2M, and the ones that work at $2M break at $10M.

Agency growth is not linear. It's a series of plateaus and inflection points. At each inflection point, the founder's role changes, the team structure changes, the sales model changes, and the delivery model changes. Founders who don't anticipate these changes hit walls.

## The Stages
Stage 1 — Solo/Duo ($0-$300K): The founder does everything. Sales, delivery, billing, marketing. The bottleneck is the founder's time. The growth lever is raising rates and improving efficiency.

Stage 2 — Small Team ($300K-$1M): 2-5 people. The founder starts delegating delivery but still owns sales and client relationships. The bottleneck shifts to hiring and process. The growth lever is building a repeatable delivery process.

Stage 3 — Studio ($1M-$3M): 5-15 people. Departments emerge. The founder becomes a manager, not a maker. The bottleneck is middle management and process consistency. The growth lever is productizing services and systematizing delivery.

Stage 4 — Agency ($3M-$10M): 15-40 people. Multiple teams, multiple service lines. The founder becomes CEO. The bottleneck is leadership, culture, and operational infrastructure. The growth lever is brand, positioning, and sales infrastructure.

Stage 5 — Scale ($10M+): 40+ people. The founder's role is vision, culture, and strategic relationships. The bottleneck is maintaining quality at volume. The growth lever is operational excellence and strategic positioning.

## When to use
When planning the next growth stage. When the current model is hitting capacity. When systems that used to work are breaking.
