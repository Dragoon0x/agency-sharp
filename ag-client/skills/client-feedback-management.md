# Client Feedback Management
**Purpose:** Collect, interpret, and act on client feedback effectively — because most client feedback is about feelings, not design, and the skill is translating between the two.

"I don't like it" is not design feedback. It's an emotional response. The agency's job is to translate that response into actionable direction: what specifically isn't working, what they expected instead, and what would make them feel confident in the direction. Most client-agency friction comes from this translation failure.

## The Feedback Collection Framework
Present work with context: "We designed this to achieve [objective]. Here's how each element supports that goal." Then ask specific questions: "Does this headline communicate the value you discussed?" "Does this visual direction feel aligned with your brand?" "Is there anything here that would concern your stakeholders?" Specific questions produce specific feedback. "What do you think?" produces vague responses.

## The Translation Skill
"Can you make it pop?" → "Which element should draw more attention?" "It feels too corporate" → "Should we explore a warmer color palette and less formal typography?" "My CEO won't like it" → "What would your CEO specifically object to? Let's address those concerns directly." Every vague piece of feedback has a specific concern underneath. Find it.

## The Consolidation Rule
Feedback must come from one voice. If three stakeholders send separate, conflicting feedback, the project stalls. Establish at the start: "Please consolidate feedback internally and send us one set of aligned feedback." This is non-negotiable. Attempting to satisfy contradictory feedback from multiple stakeholders produces work that satisfies no one.

## When to use
Every design presentation. When client feedback is vague or contradictory. When revisions are multiplying because the underlying concern isn't being addressed. When multiple stakeholders are giving conflicting direction.
