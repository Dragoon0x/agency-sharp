# Client Communication System
**Purpose:** Build communication rhythms that keep clients informed, confident, and out of your team's way — because overcommunication prevents the anxiety that causes micromanagement.

Client anxiety is the root cause of most client management problems. Anxious clients micromanage, request unnecessary meetings, send "just checking in" emails, and make last-minute changes. The antidote is proactive, predictable communication that answers their questions before they ask them.

## The Communication Cadence
Weekly status updates: sent every Friday (or Monday for the week ahead). Same format every time: what was accomplished, what's next, any blockers or decisions needed, and updated timeline. The consistency matters more than the content — clients who know when the update is coming stop sending "how's it going?" messages in between.

## The Status Update Format
Project: [Name]. Period: [Week of X]. Progress: [what was completed — be specific, include screenshots or links]. Next week: [what's planned]. Decisions needed: [anything blocking progress — with a deadline]. Timeline status: [on track / at risk / delayed — with explanation if not on track]. This update should take 15 minutes to write. If it takes longer, the project is more complex than the communication suggests.

## The Escalation Protocol
Not everything goes in the weekly update. Issues that need immediate attention: timeline risks, budget concerns, quality problems, team changes. The rule: bad news travels fast. A client who learns about a delay in week 3 is annoyed. A client who learns about it in week 1 with a recovery plan is understanding. Speed of escalation is directly proportional to client trust.

## When to use
Every client engagement. When clients are anxious or micromanaging. When the team spends too much time on ad-hoc client communication. When project issues are surfacing too late.
