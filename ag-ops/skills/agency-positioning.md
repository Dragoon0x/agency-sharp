# Agency Positioning
**Purpose:** Position the agency so the right clients find you and the wrong ones don't — because an agency that serves everyone serves no one.

Agency positioning is the most underleveraged strategic decision. Most agencies position by capability: "we do design and development." This tells clients nothing about why they should choose you over the other 10,000 agencies that do design and development. Strong positioning names the specific client, the specific problem, and the specific outcome.

## The Positioning Stack
Layer 1: Who do you serve? (industry + company stage + specific role). Layer 2: What problem do you solve? (not "we build websites" but "we help B2B SaaS companies convert free users to paid"). Layer 3: How are you different? (your method, your speed, your specific expertise). Layer 4: What proof do you have? (case studies, metrics, recognizable logos).

## The Specificity Advantage
"We're a design agency" attracts price shoppers. "We design onboarding experiences for fintech products that increase activation rates by 30-50%" attracts clients who will pay premium rates because the value is specific and measurable. Every word of specificity you add filters out bad-fit clients and attracts good-fit ones.

## The Positioning Test
Read your positioning to a stranger. Can they immediately name a company that should hire you? If the answer is "any company that needs a website," the positioning is too broad. If the answer is "a Series B SaaS company struggling with churn," the positioning is working.

## When to use
At agency founding. When growth stalls. When you're competing on price instead of value. When ideal clients aren't finding you.
