# Scrum Practice Agency
**Purpose:** Apply scrum practice agency with the rigor and specificity that profitable agency delivery demands — where client expectations, fixed budgets, and quality standards leave no room for guesswork.

## Why This Matters

Product management in an agency context means ensuring that what gets built actually solves the client's user's problem — not just what the client asked for. The agency PM bridges client business objectives, user needs, and technical feasibility, making tradeoff decisions that maximize value within budget and timeline constraints.

For scrum practice agency specifically, the gap between doing this well and doing it poorly is the gap between a profitable project and a money-losing one. Agencies that systematize scrum practice agency deliver more consistently, scope more accurately, and retain clients longer than agencies that handle it ad-hoc.

## The Framework

Prioritization for Agency Work: Impact (how much does this move the client's key metric?) × Confidence (how sure are we?) / Effort (person-days). But add an agency-specific filter: Client Priority (is this what the client cares about most?). A high-impact, low-effort feature that the client doesn't care about is politically useless.

## How to Apply This

**Step 1: Assess the current state.** Before changing anything, document how scrum practice agency is currently handled. What works? What breaks? Where does the team spend unnecessary time? The assessment reveals whether the problem is process, people, tools, or all three.

**Step 2: Define the standard.** What does good look like for scrum practice agency in this agency, for this project type, at this scale? The standard should be specific enough that two team members would independently produce similar outputs. If the standard is vague, the results will be inconsistent.

**Step 3: Build the system.** Create the templates, checklists, documentation, and workflows that make the standard repeatable. The system should be lightweight enough to actually use and comprehensive enough to catch the common failure modes.

**Step 4: Measure and improve.** Track whether the system is working. The key metrics for scrum practice agency: Sprint velocity (stories completed per sprint), scope change rate, client satisfaction per sprint, user metric improvement post-launch, time-to-launch vs planned, defect escape rate. Review quarterly. Adjust based on what the data shows, not what feels right.

## Real-World Use Cases

**Early-stage agency (1-5 people):** At this scale, scrum practice agency is often handled by the founder directly. The priority is documenting what works before the team grows. Create a simple version — a one-page checklist or template — that captures the founder's judgment so it can be delegated.

**Growth-stage agency (5-20 people):** At this scale, scrum practice agency must be delegated and systematized. The founder can't be in every loop. Build the standard, train the team, and create quality checks that catch problems without creating bottlenecks. The goal is consistent output without the founder's involvement in every instance.

**Scaled agency (20+ people):** At this scale, scrum practice agency needs governance — who owns the standard, how it's updated, how new team members are trained, and how exceptions are handled. The system should be self-maintaining: documented enough that a new PM or lead can manage it without institutional knowledge from the founders.

## Common Mistakes

**No success metrics defined before building — no way to know if the product worked.** This is the most common failure mode and the one that costs the most in rework, client friction, and margin erosion.

**Scope creep through 'small' additions — features that are 'just one more thing' that collectively double the project.** This usually happens when the agency is moving too fast to follow the process — which is exactly when the process matters most.

**Not showing work in progress — surprising the client at the end instead of aligning throughout.** This compounds over time. Each instance is small. The cumulative effect is significant.

## The Agency-Specific Dimension

Agency work has constraints that product teams and freelancers don't face. Multiple clients means scrum practice agency must work across different brands, industries, and stakeholder dynamics. Parallel projects mean the team handles scrum practice agency for 3-5 clients simultaneously. Fixed budgets mean there's no room for the process to be inefficient. External deadlines mean the timeline is real. These constraints make systematizing scrum practice agency not optional but essential.

## Metrics to Track

Sprint velocity (stories completed per sprint), scope change rate, client satisfaction per sprint, user metric improvement post-launch, time-to-launch vs planned, defect escape rate

## When to use

When scrum practice agency is the current bottleneck in delivery or operations. When quality in this area is inconsistent across projects or team members. When the agency is scaling and needs scrum practice agency to work without the founder in the loop. When post-project retrospectives identify this as a recurring issue.
